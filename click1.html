    • Overview

    The Bhagavad Gita is a sacred Hindu scripture, part of the Mahabharata, where Lord Krishna imparts divine wisdom to Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. Facing moral confusion, Arjuna hesitates to fight, and Krishna enlightens him on duty (dharma), selfless action (karma yoga), devotion (bhakti yoga), and knowledge (jnana yoga). It teaches detachment, righteousness, and the eternal nature of the soul.
